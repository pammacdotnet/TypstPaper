\section*{Affine spaces}
Let $A$ be a linear subspace of another space $V$. We say that $A$ is a Euclidean and affine linear space if there exists a mapping such that: \[ A\times A\rightarrow V \] where $A\times A$ means \emph{linear mapping that takes a tuple from} $A$.
\section*{Example}
Let the mapping $f$ take two elements $\mathbf{a}=\left(a_{x},a_{y}\right)$ y $\mathbf{b}=\left(b_{x},b_{y}\right)$ from $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{2}$ and do the following with them:
\[ f(a,b)=\left(a_{x}-b_{x},a_{y}-b_{y}\right) \]
